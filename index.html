The Concept

"レコメンドの圏外へ。
Beyond the Recommended.
Journeying to encounter
the sublime."

01

Beyond Algorithms

Not what is recommended. What is real, rare, and unrepeatable. In an era when algorithms decide what we see and who we become, MMM chooses to travel to the edges — to find the human beings whose stories have not yet been optimized away.

02

Setsuna 刹那

The Buddhist concept of impermanence — the belief that everything in this world is in a constant state of flux. Even now, our cells are regenerating; we are not the same people we were a second ago. Since no moment ever repeats, every single one is a miracle. MMM exists to capture these miraculous fragments of time.

03

Beauty as Art

Not pretty. Not cute. The moment a person's conviction and existence manifests — that is Art. MMM captures the beauty shaped by the land, the streets, the culture, and the inner philosophy that defines an individual. The world is inherently beautiful. Illuminating that beauty inspires the courage and joy to truly live.

"The same eye that reads silence in a 400-year-old cedar barrel
reads the data pulse of a city at night.
Because to me, they are asking the same question." — Daisuke Abe, Director / Medium

I am a Tokyo-based director and cinematographer specializing in immersive visual storytelling that bridges the gap between art and documentary. I work across advertising, short films, music videos, and art installations — blending storytelling with a refined visual aesthetic recognized in Japan and internationally.

To me, filmmaking is not merely an act of recording; it is an act of rebirth. Under my lens, old machinery is transformed into powerful symbolic icons, and individuals emerge with newfound confidence. I believe cinema has the power to reshape how we understand the world.

I am deeply fascinated by the intersection of environment, technology, and human experience. My commercial portfolio spans AgriTech (Oishii Farm, NY/NJ), deep tech (Telexistence, TX Motion Data), heritage craft (Kikumasamune sake, Elmes of Japan), and spatial performance — a range that is not accidental. It is a demonstration of one method applied across every register.

I am not interested in the difference between old and new. I am interested in what survives the translation — the thing that remains true when the medium changes.
